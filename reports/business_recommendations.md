# Business Recommendations

## 1. Prioritize Month-to-Month Customers

Month-to-month customers are expected to be the highest-risk group because they can leave with little friction. Offer targeted incentives to move high-value month-to-month customers into one-year plans, such as loyalty credits, free upgrades, or discounted bundles.

## 2. Build an Early-Life Retention Program

Customers with low tenure should receive proactive onboarding support. Recommended actions include welcome check-ins, first-bill reviews, service setup help, and education about included features.

## 3. Improve Payment Experience for High-Risk Billing Segments

If electronic check customers show elevated churn, the business should investigate whether billing friction, failed payments, or lower digital engagement contributes to cancellation. Encourage automatic payment enrollment with small monthly credits.

## 4. Bundle Support and Security Services

Customers without tech support, online security, or online backup may perceive less value from the provider. Bundle these services into targeted retention offers, especially for high monthly charge customers.

## 5. Segment by Monthly Charge and Tenure

High-spend, low-tenure customers should receive priority retention outreach because they represent both immediate churn risk and meaningful revenue exposure. Build a retention queue using monthly charges, tenure, contract type, and service mix.

## 6. Track Retention Campaign Performance

For every intervention, measure churn rate, renewal rate, discount cost, and retained revenue. A retention offer should be judged by incremental profit, not only reduced churn.

## Recommended KPI Dashboard

- Overall churn rate
- Churn rate by contract type
- Churn rate by tenure group
- Churn rate by payment method
- Monthly recurring revenue at risk
- Retention offer acceptance rate
- Retained revenue after campaign
